About

Established in 2021. gon tattoo studio is centered around service and community. We aim to provide a comfortable space for both our clients and artists. Our artists work as independent contractors within our studio but we work as a collective to uphold gin’s values of inclusivity.

FAQ

General Questions

  1. Where are you located?

    Gon studio is located in the heart of Queens, on the 3rd floor of 32-03 Lolly Street, Queens, NY.

  2. How do I book an appointment?

    Due to Covid-19, gon studio will be taking appointments only to comply with the CDC and the New York State guidelines to ensure we keep everyone safe. To schedule a consultation or session, please fill out the “request appointment” form. We will get back to you as soon as possible. Please be patient as we go through our inquiries.

  3. How much will the tattoo cost?

    Gon studio requires a $100 minimum. Price is based on size and complexity of the design and the hourly rate of the artists. Artists will give you an exact price during the consultation period and before the session.

  4. How do I choose my artist?

    Gon studios comprises of various artists who specilize in different styles of tattoo artwork. Feel free to explore the artists’ portfolios on the artists page or visit our Instagram.

  5. What is the cancellation policyt?

    All deposits are non-refundable and non-transferable. This deposit will go to the final price of your tattoo. If you decide to reschedule, the deposit can be credited up to 48 hours in advance. We allow up to 3 months to reschedule an appointment after the initial deposit. If it isn’t scheduled by then, the deposit will be forfeited.

  6. Do you do cover ups?

    We are more than happy to do cover ups based on your case! Send a request for consultation to discuss the design

Aftercare

  1. How do I care for my fresh tattoo?

    Keep the adhesive bandage up to 3—4 days. This adhesive is breathable and waterproof. Before taking the bandages off, wash your hands with soap. While removing the bandage, rinse the tattoo with warm water and soap. Pat down with a clean paper towel. Wash the tattoo twice a day with water and soap then apply a thin layer of “Hustle Butter” or “Aquaphor” ointment 3—4 times a day. It is essential to keep your tattoo clean and moistu

  2. What should I not do?

    Avoid soaps with fragrances or harsh chemicals.

    Avoid petroleum based ointments, such as Vaseline.

    Do not scratch or pick at the tattoo.
